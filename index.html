Runtime Safety Kernel

Deterministic veto kernel for safety-critical AI

MGOS Sentinel™ is a non-learning authorization boundary between AI inference and execution. If upstream signals conflict, execution is blocked — never averaged. Every decision produces a tamper-evident audit receipt.

⊢

Termination

Always halts. No unbounded loops, recursion, or data-dependent iteration. Bounded-time by construction.

⊥

Fail-Safe

Contradictory inputs trigger BLOCK, never pass-through. No averaging of conflicting signals into risky execution.

≡

Determinism

Identical inputs always produce identical outputs. Bitwise-reproducible decisions verified by SHA-256 hashes.

#

Audit Trail

Every decision emits a hash-verified, tamper-evident receipt. Replayable by third parties. Chain-linked witnesses.

Problem

AI systems make probabilistic decisions. Safety requires deterministic guarantees.

When multiple upstream sources disagree — sensors conflict, models hallucinate, channels go Byzantine — probabilistic guardrails average their way into unsafe execution. Content filters catch bad outputs. Nobody gates the actions.

Solution

MGOS separates prediction from authorization at the execution boundary.

AI proposes. MGOS decides. The kernel sits at the last line before actuation: no model weights, no training, no drift. Contradiction reduces available actions to zero — triggering a conservative veto with a cryptographic receipt proving why.
